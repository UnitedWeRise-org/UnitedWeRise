UnitedWeRise.org Petition Portal Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 27, 2026 | Last Updated: March 27, 2026

This policy governs the collection, use, retention, and disclosure of information gathered through the petition portal operated by UnitedWeRise.org, a project of People United for Peaceful Revolution, Inc. ("PUPR"), a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization (collectively, "UWR," "we," or "us"). This policy applies specifically to the petition portal. For UWR's general privacy policy, see our general privacy policy.

Our Commitment

UWR was conceived to protect and expand rights of speech, political association, and civic participation. This commitment extends to how we handle the data of every person who uses this platform. We do not sell your data. We do not use your data for advertising. We collect only what is necessary to operate the petition portal and to verify the authenticity of petition signatures.

Information We Collect

When you sign a petition through the UWR petition portal, the following information is collected:

Information you provide directly: Your first and last name, residence address, and any additional fields specified by the petition creator (which may include, but are not limited to, email address, phone number, and party enrollment status). You will also provide a typed signature and an affirmation acknowledging the accuracy of your submission under penalty of perjury.

Information collected automatically: Your IP address, device identifier, a timestamp of your submission, and the results of a CAPTCHA verification challenge.

Information obtained through verification services: Where applicable, your name, address, and party enrollment status may be checked against publicly available voter registration records to verify your eligibility to sign a particular petition.

How Your Information Is Used

Your information is used for the following purposes and no others:

  • To record your signature on the petition you signed.
  • To verify your identity and eligibility as a petition signer.
  • To provide the petition creator with your signature and the information you submitted.
  • To maintain an audit trail sufficient to demonstrate the authenticity of petition signatures.

Who Receives Your Information

Petition creators receive your name, address, and any additional fields you completed, along with your contact preference (see below). Petition creators do not receive your IP address, device identifier, or other technical metadata.

Third-party verification and infrastructure services. UWR uses third-party services to operate the petition portal, including cloud hosting infrastructure (currently Microsoft Azure), CAPTCHA verification (currently hCaptcha), voter registration verification services, and payment processing (currently Stripe, where applicable). These providers receive only the limited data necessary to perform their specific function and are governed by their own privacy policies and data processing agreements. UWR does not authorize these providers to use your data for any purpose other than the service they provide to UWR.

Government agencies. If a petition is submitted to a government agency as an official election filing (such as a designating petition filed with a board of elections), your name, address, party enrollment, and signature will become part of that filing. Official election filings are public records and may be subject to public inspection, challenge proceedings, and court review under applicable election law. You will be clearly informed at the time of signing if a petition is designated for official election filing.

UWR does not sell or otherwise distribute signer information to any party other than the petition creator.

Your Contact Preferences

When signing a petition, you will be asked whether you consent to being contacted by the petition creator for purposes related to the petition. This is opt-in only. If you do not affirmatively opt in, your contact information will not be provided to the petition creator for outreach purposes. UWR does not independently contact signers for purposes unrelated to the petition they signed, and UWR does not add signers to any mailing list.

UWR communicates your stated contact preference to the petition creator, but cannot guarantee the actions of third parties. See "Petition Creator Obligations" below.

Petition Creator Obligations

By creating a petition on the UWR platform, petition creators agree to the following:

  • Signer data may be used only for the stated purposes of the petition.
  • Signer contact preferences must be honored. Signers who do not opt in to contact may not be added to mailing lists, contacted for solicitation, or otherwise reached by the petition creator.
  • Signer data may not be sold, rented, or distributed to any third party not identified in the petition's stated purpose.

Violation of these obligations may result in removal from the platform or legal action.

Data Retention

Election petitions. For petitions associated with a specific election, full signature records (name, address, party enrollment, signature, affirmation, and submission timestamp) are retained for 22 months following the associated election date, consistent with federal election records preservation requirements under 52 U.S.C. § 20701. Technical metadata (IP address, device identifier) is retained through the applicable statutory challenge period following the petition filing deadline, then permanently deleted. After the 22-month retention period, election petition signature records are automatically deleted unless subject to an active legal hold.

General petitions. Data retention policies for non-election petitions are under development and will be published separately. Until such policies are finalized, general petition data will be retained in accordance with the principles stated in this policy.

Legal holds. In the event of pending or anticipated litigation, regulatory inquiry, or valid legal process, UWR may extend retention of relevant data beyond the standard retention period. Any such extension will be documented with the reason for the hold and will be lifted when the legal matter concludes.

Responses to Legal Process

We conceived this platform to protect rights of speech and association that are under threat across other platforms. We will do our best to resist government pressure, but UWR will comply with valid legal process, including subpoenas and court orders. In such cases:

  • UWR will comply only with the minimum scope required by the legal process.
  • UWR will notify the affected petition creator and, where legally permitted and feasible, affected signers before producing records.
  • UWR will challenge overbroad or legally deficient requests where resources permit.

Signer Rights

Election petitions. Signers of petitions designated for official election filing should direct any retraction requests to the petition creator. Once a petition is filed with a government agency, it is governed by applicable election law, and UWR cannot unilaterally modify a filed record.

General petitions. Signers of general petitions may request removal of their signature by contacting UWR at contact@unitedwerise.org. UWR will make reasonable efforts to locate and delete the relevant record.

Content Disclaimer

The views, statements, and purposes expressed in petitions hosted on the UWR platform are those of the petition creators and signers. UWR does not endorse, verify, or take responsibility for the content of any petition, and hosting a petition on this platform does not constitute UWR's endorsement of its purpose or claims.

Changes to This Policy

UWR may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be posted on this page with a revised "Last Updated" date. Continued use of the petition portal after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
