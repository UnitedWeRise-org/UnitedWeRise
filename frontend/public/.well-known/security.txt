# Security Vulnerability Disclosure Policy

Thank you for helping to keep UnitedWeRise and our users safe!

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability, please report it by emailing:
security@unitedwerise.org

Please include the following information in your report:
- Type of vulnerability (e.g., XSS, SQL injection, authentication bypass)
- Full paths of affected source files (if known)
- Location of the affected code (URL or file path)
- Step-by-step instructions to reproduce the issue
- Proof-of-concept or exploit code (if possible)
- Impact of the vulnerability and how it might be exploited

## Our Commitment

- We will acknowledge your email within 48 hours
- We will provide a detailed response within 7 days
- We will work with you to understand and validate the vulnerability
- We will keep you informed as we work on a fix
- We will credit you in our security acknowledgments (unless you prefer to remain anonymous)

## Scope

In-scope vulnerabilities include:
- Authentication and authorization bypass
- SQL injection, XSS, CSRF
- Remote code execution
- Sensitive data exposure
- Server-side request forgery (SSRF)
- Insecure cryptographic storage or transmission
- Security misconfigurations with demonstrable impact

Out-of-scope:
- Denial of Service (DoS) attacks
- Social engineering attacks against staff or users
- Physical attacks against our infrastructure
- Issues affecting outdated/unsupported browsers
- Theoretical vulnerabilities without proof-of-concept

## Safe Harbor

We consider security research conducted under this policy to be:
- Authorized under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)
- Authorized under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)
- Exempt from restrictions in our Terms of Service that would interfere with security research
- Lawful and helpful to the security of the internet

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:
- Follow this disclosure policy
- Avoid violating privacy or destroying data
- Report vulnerabilities promptly
- Do not exploit vulnerabilities beyond demonstrating them
- Act in good faith

## Recognition

We maintain a Security Hall of Fame on our website to recognize researchers who have
helped us improve security. Please let us know if you would like to be included.
